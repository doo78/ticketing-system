Frequently Asked Questions

Account Management

You can reset your password through the university's self-service portal. Visit University Helpdesk and click on "Forgot Password." You will need to verify your identity using your registered email or phone number.

If you're unable to access the self-service portal, you can visit the helpdesk in person with your university ID for immediate assistance.

New students and faculty should have received an email with activation instructions to their personal email address. Follow the link in that email to set up your account.

If you haven't received the activation email within 48 hours of your official start date, please contact the helpdesk with your university ID number and we will assist you with the account setup process.

The university requires multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all accounts. To set it up:

  1. Log into the university portal
  2. Go to "Account Settings" and select "Security"
  3. Click "Set up Multi-Factor Authentication"
  4. Follow the prompts to add your mobile device or other authentication methods

We recommend setting up multiple authentication methods (e.g., mobile app and SMS) to ensure you always have access to your account.


Network & Connectivity

To connect to the university Wi-Fi:

  1. Go to your device's Wi-Fi settings
  2. Select the "Universty wifi" network
  3. When prompted, enter your university email and password
  4. Accept the security certificate if prompted

For devices that don't support WPA2-Enterprise authentication, use the "University_Guest" network and register your device through the captive portal.

Several factors can affect Wi-Fi performance on campus:

  • High-density areas during peak hours may experience congestion
  • Distance from the nearest access point
  • Interference from other electronic devices
  • Building materials blocking signals in certain areas

If you consistently experience slow connections in specific locations, please report this to the helpdesk with details about the time, location, and your device. This helps us improve our network coverage.


Software & Applications

The university provides a wide range of software applications for academic and administrative use. To access these:

  1. Visit the Software Portal at software.university.edu
  2. Log in with your university credentials
  3. Browse available software by category or use the search function
  4. Follow the installation instructions for your specific operating system

Many applications are also available through virtual desktop infrastructure, which you can access from any device without installation.

You can access your university email in several ways:

  • Web browser: Visit mail.university.edu and log in with your university credentials
  • Desktop client: Configure Outlook or other email clients using the settings available on our support page
  • Mobile devices: Use the Outlook app or add your account to your device's native email app

For setup instructions on specific devices, please visit our email configuration guide on the helpdesk website.

For issues with the Learning Management System (LMS):

  1. Clear your browser cache and cookies
  2. Try using a different browser (we recommend Chrome or Firefox)
  3. Check if the LMS is experiencing an outage on our service status page
  4. For course-specific access issues, contact your instructor first
  5. For technical problems, contact the helpdesk with specific error messages and screenshots

We also offer LMS training sessions for students and faculty throughout the semester. Check the event calendar for upcoming sessions.


Hardware & Equipment

Yes, the university offers equipment loans for academic purposes:

  • Laptops: Available for short-term (daily) or long-term (semester) loans
  • Tablets: Available for up to one week
  • Projectors & Presentation Equipment: Available for classroom or event use
  • Specialized Equipment: Department-specific equipment may be available through your academic department

To borrow equipment, visit the Equipment Loan desk at the main library or submit a request through the online portal at least 24 hours in advance.

Printing services are available at multiple locations across campus:

  • Main Library (all floors)
  • Student Union Building
  • Computer labs in each academic building
  • Residence hall common areas

To print, you can:

  1. Use a campus computer and print directly
  2. Upload your document to print.university.edu
  3. Send an email with your document to print@university.edu
  4. Use the mobile printing app available for iOS and Android

All printing requires your university ID card for release and charges apply to your printing account.

For university-issued device repairs:

  1. Back up your important data if possible
  2. Submit a repair ticket through the helpdesk portal
  3. Bring your device to the Hardware Support Center during business hours
  4. For emergency loaners, indicate this need in your ticket

Typical repair times range from same-day service for minor issues to 3-5 business days for major repairs. You'll receive email updates on the status of your repair.

Note: For personally-owned devices, we offer limited troubleshooting assistance but cannot perform hardware repairs.
